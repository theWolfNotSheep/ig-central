# Governance Hub — Browse Pack Library — Demo Script

**Duration: 2:30**

**Goal:** Show how a records manager can stand up a fully-formed governance framework in minutes by browsing and importing curated packs from the Hub — instead of building one from scratch.

## Script

1. **Open the product.** *"One of the hardest parts of any IG programme isn't the technology — it's deciding what your taxonomy should be, what retention periods apply, which PII patterns matter in your jurisdiction. Most organisations spend six to nine months just agreeing on those basics. Let me show you how we short-circuit that."*

2. **Navigate to Governance → Hub** from the sidebar. *"This is the Governance Hub — think of it as an app store for ready-made governance frameworks. Packs are authored by records management specialists, legal teams, and industry bodies, and they drop straight into your tenant."*

3. **Walk the pack listing.** *"You'll see packs grouped by domain — UK public sector, NHS records, US HIPAA, Australian Privacy Act, ISO 15489 generic. Each card shows the pack name, publisher, version, and what's inside — how many categories, how many retention rules, how many PII patterns."*

4. **Show the filters.** *"Filter by jurisdiction, by industry, by author. If you're a UK local authority, you narrow to UK public sector and immediately see the retention schedules aligned to the Records Retention Schedule your sector already follows."*

5. **Hover the badges.** *"The version badge tells you what's current. The component badges tell you what you're getting — taxonomy, sensitivity levels, retention, metadata schemas, PII patterns, policies."*

6. **Click into a pack** (e.g. "ISO 15489 Generic"). *"We'll go deep into pack details in the next walkthrough — for now, note how much is waiting for you here. This pack alone brings you twelve top-level categories, forty-plus subcategories, a complete retention schedule, and the PII patterns aligned to UK GDPR. That's weeks of work you don't have to do."*

**Key message:** *"Your records team stops drafting taxonomies in Excel and starts picking the one that already fits your regulator. Day one, you have a framework your auditors will recognise."*
