# Governance Hub — Pack Diff & Conflict Detection — Demo Script

**Duration: 2:30**

**Goal:** Show that when a pack updates clash with local customisations, the platform surfaces the conflict clearly and lets the admin resolve on their terms — not automatically overwriting local work.

## Script

1. **Set up the problem.** *"Customers customise. Your records manager adds a local retention rule, tweaks a sensitivity description, adds a subcategory specific to your business. Then the Hub publishes a pack update. What happens to your customisation?"*

2. **Open an update with conflicts.** Navigate to Governance → Updates and click an update flagged with a conflict badge. *"This pack update has three conflicts — components where the new version differs from your local customisation."*

3. **Walk a side-by-side diff.** *"For each conflicted component, you see three columns — the original pack version you imported, your local version, and the incoming update. Changes are highlighted inline. No guesswork."*

4. **Category diff example.** *"Here, you extended 'HR > Employment Contracts' with a 'Contractor Agreements' subcategory. The new pack version adds 'Apprenticeship Contracts' at the same level. No conflict — both land. But the sensitivity default on the parent changed from INTERNAL to CONFIDENTIAL — that's a conflict, you chose INTERNAL deliberately."*

5. **Show resolution options per component.** *"For every conflict you have three choices — Keep local, Take pack version, or Merge. Merge opens a visual editor where you can cherry-pick fields from both sides."*

6. **Demonstrate bulk resolution.** *"Large update? Resolve everything with defaults — 'keep all local customisations' or 'take all pack changes' — then override the few that need attention."*

7. **Finish with the audit record.** *"Every conflict, every resolution, every reason is written to the audit log. Your next ISO audit, you can demonstrate exactly how you responded to every upstream change."*

**Key message:** *"You get the benefit of continuously updated frameworks without losing a single local customisation — with full evidence of every decision you made."*
